Thank you for applying for a cultivation license with CalCannabis. This letter is to notify you that your application, [ALT ID], has been approved and the license fee has been paid in full.

Pursuant to Business and Professions Code section 26050.2, you have been issued a Provisional Cannabis Cultivation License. Provisional licenses are non-renewable and valid for 12 months.

Your official cultivation license can be printed by logging in to your account on the CalCannabis Civic Portal. To access your license:

  • From the menu bar select My Licenses
  • Locate License Number # [PARENT RECORD ID]
  • Click on License #
  • Click on Record Info
  • Click on Attachments
  • Select the license from the list of attachments

California Code of Regulations, Title 3, Division 8, Chapter 1 requires that your license be prominently displayed on the licensed premises where it can be viewed by state or local agencies.

California Cannabis Track-and-Trace (CCTT) System Access: If the Designated Responsible Party for this license has not already registered for and participated in the required CCTT system training, the Designated Responsible Party must register and attend this required training prior to being credentialed as the Licensee’s initial track-and-trace account manager. Access to the CCTT system will not be granted until this training is completed. To register for CCTT system training, please access the following URL: metrc.com/California.




If the Designated Responsible Party for the license has already registered and participated in the required training, please contact the Franwell support desk at telephone number 1-877-566-6506 to establish an initial login to the CCTT system.

The Designated Responsible Party will be required to agree to system usage terms and conditions, and provide an attestation upon initial CCTT system log-in. If the Designated Responsible Party intends to delegate primary responsibility for updating and maintaining the Licensee’s cannabis distribution chain information in the CCTT system to another owner or employee in the Licensee’s organization, the Designated Responsible Party will also need to establish that individual as the track-and-trace account manager within the CCTT system. The track-and-trace account manager is responsible for establishing and maintaining logins and permissions for any other Licensee employees that require access to the CCTT system and providing those users training in how to use the CCTT system.

Within 30 business days of receipt of required unique identifiers (UIDs) ordered by the Licensee, the Licensee is responsible for following the requirements found in California Code of Regulations, title 3, section 8407, if applicable.

Questions regarding this notice may be directed to CalCannabis Licensing staff via telephone at (833)-CAL-GROW (225-4769) or via email at calcannabislicensing@cdfa.ca.gov. Please do not send confidential information to this email account.
